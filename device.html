The WeeCare Sensor

A paper-thin, battery-free NFC sensor sticker that transforms any incontinence pad into a smart, precision diagnostic tool — automatically tracking fluid volume with clinical-grade accuracy.

Explore the Design View App Mockups
Anatomy
Inside the Sensor
A flexible NFC sticker with two functional zones: the antenna module for wireless power and communication, and the impedance-sensing array for precision moisture measurement.
1

NFC Antenna Coil

Multi-turn spiral antenna harvests RF energy from a smartphone's NFC field. Powers the entire sensor — no battery required.

2

Impedance Sensing Electrodes

Interdigitated copper traces detect changes in electrical impedance as moisture is absorbed, measuring volume with precision to 50 mL.

3

Signal Routing Traces

Low-resistance copper interconnects carry impedance data from the sensing array to the NFC IC for digitization and transmission.

4

Medical-Grade Adhesive

Hypoallergenic, skin-safe adhesive backing attaches securely to any standard incontinence pad. Peel-and-stick application.

Application
Three Simple Steps
Designed so anyone — patients, caregivers, or clinical staff — can apply the sensor in seconds.
1

Peel the Backing

Remove the protective backing to expose the medical-grade adhesive underneath.

2

Place on Any Pad

Position the sensor strip on the inside surface of any standard incontinence pad or underwear.

3

Press Firmly — Done

Press for 5 seconds to secure. The sensor begins working immediately — no pairing or setup needed.

Technology
How It Works
From moisture detection to clinical insight — fully automated, fully passive.

Moisture Detected

Impedance changes across the electrode array as fluid contacts the sensor surface.

NFC Data Transfer

Phone's NFC field powers the sensor and reads impedance data wirelessly. No battery needed.

App Logs Automatically

WeeCare calculates fluid volume, timestamps the event, and adds it to the digital bladder diary.

Clinical Insight

Clinicians receive a complete, objective bladder diary — no patient recall required.
